## 1. How the relationship began (2022–2023)
- BCH opened as an EMotorad dealer under the **Bharath Cycle Centre** name (entity Inkodop; Tally opening 01-Apr-2023: ₹3,20,023 + a **₹3,50,000 security deposit** "Chanakya"). Sales ran through the **Centre** folio and ICICI receipts.
- Early friction was already visible: damaged Lil-E / yellow scooter forks on arrival (May-2023), a ₹78,778 credit + missing bills SP/394 & SP/395 never cleanly resolved, and a **Paytail ₹5,000 short** escalated to finance and never confirmed closed.
- The bond with EM's leadership was genuine and personal — Sandeep Sir mentored Syed on store experience, MSD-birthday activations, growth VCs. Syed repeatedly calls EM *"our bread and butter,"* *"a very personal brand."*

## 2. The growth years and the first cracks (2024)
- Volume scaled hard — BCH became the **highest-volume dealer in South** (191 primary in a month; 342 over Apr+May 2026). EM extended real support: ~₹38 L spend on BCH (EM's own claim), manpower money, marketing, schemes.
- But **service problems set in and never left.** From the very first complaint (24-Feb-2024, a disc-brake bolt that took 16 days and 3 chases to get a reply) the pattern was fixed: **nothing moves until BCH chases; everything moves only when leadership is tagged.**
  - Jun-2024: a 24-case backlog — EM's own audit found only 12 ever ticketed, 4 resolved.
  - Aug-2024: both warranty-replacement frames arrived **damaged in transit.**
  - Oct-2024: **"30% of complaints have no ticket number"**; CEO reprimand.
  - Dec-2024: new boxed bike with a bent fork; replacement arrived missing its mudguard.
- **The accounting split begins.** In Sept-2024 EM opened a second folio — **Bharath Cycle Hub (code 666)** — and started posting new invoices there, while payments and credit notes kept landing in **Bharath Cycle Centre (code 121)**.

## 3. The reconciliation breakdown — the core financial story
This is the heart of the "years of miscalculation."

- **What EM's own ledgers show:**
  - **BCC (Centre):** opening ₹23.6 L Dr (Apr-2024) → driven *negative* to **−₹25 L (credit/advance)** by Dec-2024 as payments + ₹74 L of credit notes piled in with few invoices → then **₹31 L of invoices dumped back in Jan-2025**, swinging it to +₹3.77 L → settled to **₹3,48,345.92 Dr** and went dormant after Jul-2025.
  - **BCH (Hub):** opening ₹0 (Sep-2024) → **₹1,03,38,282 Dr** by 6-Jan-2025, on ₹1.53 Cr invoiced but only ₹48 L paid (the rest of the money was sitting in BCC).

Payments got mis-posted (the recurring *"₹10 L direct payment not recorded,"* *"hub payment showing in centre"*). The **EM BIZ app balance never matched reality** (disputed Feb-2025, Apr-2025). Syed spent enormous time reconciling instead of selling.
- **The 13-Jan-2025 instruction** (from Darshan): cancel all open SOs, route all new orders + service billing through **BCC until BCC hits zero**, then maintain on Hub. *Confirm this was actually honoured.*

## 5. The service crisis — the real reason for de-growth
BCH's own Service Review deck (built from the service group) is the most damning document in the entire record. Across **24-Feb-2024 → 12-Jun-2026**:
- **711 chase messages**, peaking at **225 in one quarter** (Q3-2025).
- **Average resolution 8 days against a 48-hour promise**; worst cases **88 days (throttle #81618), 80 days (motor #84001), 55 days (replacement cycles)** — up to **44× the promised TAT**.
- **The root cause, found Sep-2025:** EM agents' Zoho ticket comments were set to **"private"** — invisible to BCH for ~2 years. That single setting explains the entire two-year chase pattern.
- **Recurring product defects:** mechanical-brake failures (pads dead at **527 km**, the same on Ranger/X1/Viper), X1 controller (**30-35 cases/month**), disc-to-motor screw loosening, Trex Air/Legend display E06, harness loose at production, a **whole T-Rex+ lot missing headset spacers** (unsellable), DOA/out-of-box defects, even **replacement spares arriving defective.**
- **Warranty walked back:** a Doodle battery/range fix committed on a Google Meet became *"we'll correct the manual"*; display warranty discovered to be **6 months only at the point of denial**; a battery claim denied over a *"tampered sticker."*
- **What it cost BCH:** **1,800+ free services** (neither customer nor brand paid), ~₹700/delivery and ₹1,000/Desire-kit absorbed, plus 3 telecallers + a content team carried on thin margins.
- **It went legal/public:** a **consumer-court case filed 23-Jul-2025**; and as recently as **16-Jun-2026**, customer **Rajita Nair (Viper)** escalated a manufacturing defect (oil leak, brakes failing) **by email to CEO Kunal and a LinkedIn post** after EM sent the wrong product and went silent for 10 days.

## 6. Supply & the Viper problem (2026)
- 2026's flagship **Viper** became a fresh wound: BCH took **38+ pre-bookings**, but EM could not supply until **end-July 2026** (container/"war" issues), forcing refunds and cancellations. The **01-May-2026 dispatch** arrived 40% short — and of 46 bikes, **23 were never ordered.**
- Chronic stock-outs (Doodle, Ranger, Military Blue, Kalki) repeatedly cost confirmed sales. **"X1 black with X2 display"** (10 units) has been pending *"since long."*

## 7. The strategic decline — and Syed's own diagnosis (10-Mar-2026)
In a long, candid message to Sandeep, Syed laid out why a once-booming counter de-grew **~45% YoY**:
- Top salespeople (Sunil, Govind) left → ₹1.62 Cr of sales walked out; influencer spend crashed ~90%; outside-Bangalore deliveries halved; ticket size dropped ~28%; no new models for 6-8 months; the promised **lead-generation system never worked**; EM's own **Amazon/Flipkart listings undercut the store** ("damaging the trust"); and customers could no longer get service at other EM outlets without 200% charges.
- Crucially: *"EMotorad as a brand is growing, but we as a retailer are left behind… I'm not afraid of numbers, but I don't see a system to sell those now."*
